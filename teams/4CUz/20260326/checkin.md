**Look back: peer review**

### **What worked when people used your product?**

People immediately resonated with the core problem and value proposition. Users who had experience planning group trips quickly understood the need for a centralized space and reacted positively to the idea of replacing chaotic group chats with a structured workflow.

The most compelling parts were:

* Seeing availability visualized clearly instead of coordinating manually  
* The idea of voting to reach decisions faster  
* Turning scattered ideas into a shared itinerary everyone contributes to

People especially liked that it removed the burden from a single “planner” and made the process feel collaborative.

---

### **What confused them or broke?**

Some users were unclear about:

* Where to start in the flow (what’s the first action after joining a trip?)  
* How the app transitions from ideas → decisions → finalized itinerary  
* The difference between features (e.g., voting vs. itinerary building felt slightly overlapping)

There was also uncertainty around:

* Whether this replaces or complements tools like group chats  
* How active participation from all members is enforced (vs. passive users)

---

### **What's the biggest gap?**

The biggest gap is clarity in the end-to-end flow.

While each feature (voting, scheduling, itinerary, expenses) is valuable on its own, users don’t yet fully see:

* How everything connects into a single, seamless planning experience  
* How the product ensures the group actually reaches a final decision and plan

---

### **Ship one improvement based on what you heard**

Add a clear, guided flow (progress-based UX) that shows:

* Step 1: Add availability  
* Step 2: Vote on destination  
* Step 3: Vote on activities  
* Step 4: Generate itinerary

Could be implemented as:

* A progress bar or stepper  
* A “Next step” call-to-action  
* Auto-prompts nudging users toward decisions

---

## **Look forward: your roadmap**

### **What do we still need to learn about our product?**

* Will users actually adopt a new tool instead of staying in group chats?  
* Which feature delivers the most immediate value:  
  * Scheduling?  
  * Voting?  
  * Itinerary generation?  
  * Expense tracking?  
* What drives engagement from all members, not just the organizer?  
* At what point in the planning process do users feel the most friction?

---

### **What do we need to build between now and Demo Day?**

Focus on a tight, end-to-end MVP flow:

1. Trip creation \+ invite flow  
2. Availability input \+ visualization  
3. Destination/activity voting  
4. Simple itinerary generation (even if semi-manual)  
5. Clean, shareable final plan view

Plus:

* A clear onboarding or guided experience  
* Basic UI polish to communicate structure and clarity  
* One “wow” moment (e.g., auto-generated itinerary from votes)

---

### **What's our team's biggest open question?**

How do we ensure equal participation across the group?

More specifically:

* How do we prevent one person from doing everything?  
* What mechanisms encourage or require input from everyone?  
* How do we handle inactive users without blocking progress?

---

### **What does success look like on May 12?**

Success is:

* A working prototype that demonstrates the full planning flow end-to-end  
* Users can go from trip idea → final itinerary in one session  
* The value is immediately clear without explanation  
* Demo viewers say: *“I would actually use this with my friends.”*

Strong signals:

* Users understand the flow without guidance  
* They recognize how it replaces multiple tools  
* They see how it reduces chaos and decision fatigue

Bonus:

* At least a few real users testing it with actual trip scenarios

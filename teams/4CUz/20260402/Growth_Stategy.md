**Channels you’re targeting**

1. Reddit posts under travel-related threads and Columbia student communities  
2. Asking travel-specific Discord communities or student group chats (clubs, class groups, etc)  
3. Finding random Columbia students on campus (library, CS lounge, dining halls, etc)

**Approach for each**

1. Reddit  
   1. Say: “How do you all plan trips with friends without it turning into chaos? I’ve been building a tool to fix this and curious what’s worked for people.”  
   2. Offer: Early testers; ask for feedback instead of pushing product  
   3. Target volume: 3–5 high-quality posts, 10–20 thoughtful comments engaging with others  
2. Discord  
   1. Say: “Does anyone else hate planning trips in group chats. I’m building something to organize everything (votes, plans, costs) in one place. Looking for a few people to try it for their next trip\!”  
   2. Offer: Early access; ability to influence features  
   3. Target volume: 5–10 groups, 1–2 posts per group  
3. Columbia students  
   1. Say: “Hey, quick question, have you ever planned a trip with friends and it turned into a mess in group chats? I’m building something called Tripable that organizes everything from voting, itinerary, splitting costs, etc., all in one place. I’m looking for a few Columbia students to try it for their next trip.”  
   2. Offer: Early access; direct input on features; possibly small incentive (raffle, coffee gift card, etc.)  
   3. Target volume: 10–20 conversations

**What success looks like**

* If our user count increases/more signups  
* There’s a lot of engagement/reactions to posts/comments in the thread/community  
* People are actually willing to use it and share with their friends/family  
* Qualitative feedback (pain points, feature requests)
